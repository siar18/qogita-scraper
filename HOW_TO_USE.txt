====================================================
  QOGITA SCRAPER — HOW TO USE
====================================================

WHAT DOES THIS TOOL DO?
------------------------
This scraper looks up your products on Qogita by EAN code,
finds all sellers and their prices, and gives you up to 3
suggested selling prices with profit calculations.
The result is saved as an Excel file in the output/ folder.


----------------------------------------------------
STEP 1 — SETUP (only do this once)
----------------------------------------------------
Make sure config.json exists in the qogita-scraper folder.
It should look like this:

  {
    "qogita_email": "your@email.com",
    "qogita_password": "yourpassword",
    "google_sheet_url": "https://docs.google.com/...",
    "margin_divisor": 1.12,
    "headless": true,
    "anthropic_api_key": "sk-...",
    "min_stock": 6,
    "low_stock_gap_threshold": 0.10
  }

- qogita_email / qogita_password : your Qogita login
- google_sheet_url                : link to your Google Sheet (exported as .xlsx)
- margin_divisor                  : your markup (1.12 = 12% margin)
- headless                        : true = browser runs in background (recommended)
- anthropic_api_key               : your Anthropic API key (for Claude fallback)
- min_stock                       : seller 1 stock below this triggers Scenario C (default: 6)
- low_stock_gap_threshold         : minimum price gap % to trigger Scenario C (default: 0.10 = 10%)


----------------------------------------------------
STEP 2 — OPEN THE TERMINAL IN VS CODE
----------------------------------------------------
In VS Code, click on the Terminal at the bottom.
Then navigate to the project folder by typing:

  cd /Users/siar/Projects/qogita-scraper

You only need to do this once per session.
IMPORTANT: on Mac always use "python3", not "python".


----------------------------------------------------
OPTION A — RUN FROM YOUR OWN EXCEL FILE (new products)
----------------------------------------------------
Use this when you have a list of new products in an Excel file.

Command:
  python3 run_excel.py /path/to/your_file.xlsx

Example:
  python3 run_excel.py /Users/siar/Desktop/new_products.xlsx

Test run (only first 10 products):
  python3 run_excel.py /Users/siar/Desktop/new_products.xlsx --limit 10

YOUR EXCEL FILE MUST HAVE A HEADER ROW with at least:
  - An EAN/GTIN column
      Accepted column names: EAN, GTIN, Barcode, Artikel, Article, EAN13, EAN-13
  - A cost price column
      Accepted column names: Inkoopprijs, Cost Price, Cost, Purchase Price, Costprice, Inkoop

Optional column (defaults to 0.0 if missing):
  - Your selling price
      Accepted column names: Verkoopprijs, Price, Your Price, Unit Price, Jouw Prijs, Selling Price

Column names are case-insensitive. Dutch and English both work.

Example Excel layout:
  | EAN           | Inkoopprijs | Verkoopprijs |
  | 8710400000001 | 12.50       | 18.00        |
  | 8710400000002 | 9.00        |              |


----------------------------------------------------
OPTION B — RUN FROM GOOGLE SHEETS (full inventory)
----------------------------------------------------
Use this to run the analysis on your complete product list
from the Google Sheet linked in config.json.

Command:
  python3 run_sheets.py

Test run (only first 10 products):
  python3 run_sheets.py --limit 10


----------------------------------------------------
OUTPUT — EXCEL COLUMNS EXPLAINED
----------------------------------------------------
The result is saved automatically in the output/ folder.
Filename example: output/2026-04-15_qogita_products_analysis.xlsx

The Excel has colour-coded sections:

  GREY — Product Info
    GTIN, Product Name, Cost Price, Your Current Price,
    Current Profit € / %  (profit you make right now)

  WHITE — Seller Data (what Qogita shows)
    Seller 1 (cheapest), their max price, stock, delivery time
    Seller 2 (second cheapest), their max price, stock

  BLUE — Scenario A: Be the cheapest seller (always calculated)
    Suggested Price  = Seller 1 max price / margin divisor
    Profit € / %     = profit you make at that price
    Will You Be Cheapest? = Yes/No
    Notes            = Lower needed / Price increase possible / Optimal price / Can't compete

  ORANGE — Scenario B: Seller 1 has slow delivery (only shown when applicable)
    When the cheapest seller shows an "Estimated delivery" time,
    buyers may prefer a faster supplier even at a higher price.
    Suggested Price  = Seller 2 max price / margin divisor (you become 2nd cheapest)
    Profit € / %     = profit at that price
    Seller 1 Delivery = the raw delivery text from Qogita (e.g. "7 weeks")
    Notes            = context about why this scenario applies

  YELLOW — Scenario C: Seller 1 has very low stock (only shown when applicable)
    When cheapest seller has fewer than min_stock units (default: 6) AND
    the price gap between seller 1 and seller 2 is more than 10%,
    you can price just under seller 2 and wait for seller 1 to sell out.
    When they sell out, you automatically become the cheapest seller.
    Suggested Price  = Seller 2 max price × 0.98 (2% under seller 2)
    Profit € / %     = profit at that price
    Seller 1 Stock Gap % = how much cheaper seller 1 is vs seller 2
    Notes            = seller 1 stock count + gap + strategy description

Color coding in Scenario A Notes cell:
  Yellow  = Lower needed          (you need to drop your price)
  Blue    = Price increase possible (you can raise your price)
  Green   = Optimal price         (you are right on target)
  Red     = Can't compete         (price would be below your cost)
  Grey    = Not found             (product not listed on Qogita)
  Orange  = Extraction failed     (scrape error, try again)


----------------------------------------------------
RESUME / CHECKPOINT
----------------------------------------------------
If the scraper is interrupted mid-run, it saves its progress
to checkpoint.json. The next time you run it, it will
automatically continue from where it left off and skip
products already processed.
